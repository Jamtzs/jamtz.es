Trueque App.

Swap activities from home during the lockdown.
The Project

Trueque App is a social platform where participants create a profile to offer a service in exchange for receiving another. This app allows in times of crisis to create a network of entertainment and support among the skills that each person has to offer. It is a platform to create a barter/swap community of different knowledge, activities or skills. You can search the services that other people are willing to share and find the perfect "Trueque".

Duration: 1 Months.

Role: UX/UI Designer, Team Leader.

Methods: User personas, Customer flow, Research, Wireframing, UI Design, Low and Mid - fidelity prototypes.

Tools: Figma, Asana.

Context

What's happening today?

We are living at a unique stage in the history of humanity, we have been forced to lock ourselves up and pause certain freedoms for the sake of our families. But that does not mean that we have to be bored at home, we must take advantage of the time that never stops and do those things that we have been postponing.

The challenger
How might we design useful and charity services to connect with other people at this lock-down and avoid feel bored or depressed?

While doing research on various platforms, we found they mostly provide a similar user interface and user experience solutions but, usually the user needs good quality devices.

We found that depending on which potential countries could use the software, the power plant's energy model or other information based on the time. The software must have to adapt to all devices in the market; principally the low cost and old versions.

IDEATE

User Personas

We spent most of the first day pitching our ideas to each other and brainstorming new solutions. We had a few extreme idea switches before finally choosing one that we all believed. We began to build a general user persona that includes all the needs of the moment and basic architecture for the product idea.

We proposed to create Trueque App - it is a perfect solution for the lockdown where the participant can swap experience in a simple way and stay in contact with the community.

INTERACTION DESIGN

Prototype

Sitemap.
With these core features in mind, we did a card sort to gain insight as to how users might expect the content to be organised and displayed on the platform. We used the results to create a sitemap.

IDEATE

Wirefrime

Low Fidelity Wireframes.
Since simplicity and ease of use are one of the biggest aims of Trueque App. We started created multiple versions of each screen until I found a combination of features and elements that we thought matched the users needs and that would be as intuitive as much as possible.

We then created medium-fidelity versions of these wireframes in Figma, created a clickable prototype and they helped us gain insight into what users found useful and completely change what they weren't reacting too well.

PROTOTYPE

High fidelity

Design Changes.
We made changes based on the user feedback from the prototype and anothers designers. We created a newer, high-fidelity version of the app that featured more white space, larger icons and clearer text.

To this design, I applied the Gestalt Principles, colour theory and made use of grids to ensure consistency within the entire app.

Color Palette.

Trueque App uses colour in order to make the user’s interaction easier and more predictable. It is used to assist in navigation, highlight important information, and create a great user experience.

Typography.

Trueque's typography aims for minimalism and organic design.
The app uses Roboto font in all content within the app. Make use of bold for headings, and use sparingly for emphasis

Iconography.

Icons should be used in order to communicate or enhance an action. They should be designed and used with purpose with the aim to assist.

Icon style.

For this project, we have chosen to use the library "Streamline icons." It is one of the most complete icon libraries on the market and giving us many options to represent the functions of the app.

Grid.

5 column grid should be applied in order to improve the consistency of elements on each screen.
