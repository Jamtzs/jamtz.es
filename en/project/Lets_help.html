The Project

Let’s help together

It is an ONG app that we designed as part of a 7-day UX challenge by the University of Elisava. we were given a project brief based on actual ONG and some tasks to complete daily. At the end of the 7 days, we had to have the basic outlines of an application where the client could regain confidence in different organizations.

Duration: 7 Months.
Methods: Customer flow, Solution Sketching, Wireframing, UI Design, Low - fidelity prototypes.
Tools: Figma, Marvel.
Context

We are in a decade where everyone wants help, feeling that our actions serve a purpose, a reason and in a way we believe that way we left our footprint in the memory of history.

All the organizations know it! and they ask for help to bring life to some projects So, How can we convert this necessity into an opportunity when any else believe the ONG?

We discover than a huge number of people distrust organizations for different reasons: corruption case, ideologic problems, malpractice, no transparency, etc. But the main one is the continuous negative publicity that makes you feel that your actions don't have an impact. in consequence, they prefer to look for another option and avoid to feel tied up and manipulated.

IDEATE

Wireflows

The second and third day, we began to tackle potential solutions to our users problem. First, we spent some time researching existing products and websites within the OMG industry and I reviewed their existing problems and solutions. Next, we spent 8 minutes creating 8 rough sketches with the goal to push beyond my existing ideas. The third task was about focusing and refining some ideas created in the crazy 8’s session.

CREATE

Mid-
Wirefrimes

The wireframes used to layout content and functionality on the app takes into account user needs and which object is to make an easy flow and avoid the change of option by the client.

On the fourth day, we focused on creating wireframes that would convey the ideas more clearly. we kept the design minimal and consistent, in addition to using grids and hierarchy guidelines to match everything with logic.

CREATE

High-
Wireframes

On the fifth and sixth days, we focused on high-quality version screens. We wanted to use this task to focus on accessibility and usability.

1 - we used icons and specify the colour range to help support text and show users whether their tasks were successful.

2 - We also use indicators to help users identify their place on the screen and determine how they want to organize information that they would like to know.

VALIDATE

Testing UI

In the last days, the team focused on analyzing all UI design elements and the interaction with each other screens. We chose to use purple colour, which is commonly associated with peace, pride and dignity. Also, makes a good contrast with the pastel blue colour in the background and a few some elements.

CREATE

Final product

By the end of the week-long challenge, I had designed screens that would allow my user to search for her favourite project, feel useful and comfortable with his action which will be traducing as unconditional support for the cause.

Help another without login

Many like to help others but from anonymity, if you are one of them. We have an option for you where you don't need to register and if you change your mind you still have the social network option.

Choose your cause

We know that many like to participate in community activities, maybe things about your neighbourhood or cause which match with your values and beliefs so, you can set the patron of search in a few minutes though good filters.

Control your donation

Transparency is the most important thing today. We believe that you should know in real-time, what is happening in your donations and if they have the impact that should have.
